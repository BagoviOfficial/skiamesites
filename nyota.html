Background & Context

Under the NYOTA Government Program, approximately 70 beneficiaries in Kotulo Ward will each receive:

  • KES 22,000 – First Tranche
  • KES 22,000 – Second Tranche (after business report)
  • Total: KES 44,000 per beneficiary

While this support is important, starting 70 individual businesses with small capital in the same ward presents serious challenges.

The Challenge

Why many small businesses fail

Low Capital

KES 22,000 limits stock, scale, and growth.

High Competition

70 similar businesses in one ward.

No Bargaining Power

Retail buying, retail selling.

High Failure Rate

Most micro-businesses collapse early.

The Proposed Idea

A Voluntary Peer-Based Cooperative

Beneficiaries explore pooling their first tranche to form a stronger, better-capitalized joint business that reduces competition, shares risk, and improves sustainability.

  • Voluntary participation
  • No forced pooling
  • Transparent governance
  • NYOTA compliant
